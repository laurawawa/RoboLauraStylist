# Sources — the stylists & critics RoboLaura is trained on

This file is the agent's "training roster." Each source has: where to crawl,
how reliably we can read it, the principles to extract, and how much weight to
give it. Apply everything here *through* `personal-style-guide.md`, which remains
the hard buying filter.

Access tiers:
- **Open** — reliably readable via web fetch (blogs, Substacks, listing pages).
- **Partial** — sometimes readable; use accessible posts/metadata only.
- **Hard** — usually blocked in automation (TikTok, Instagram, ShopMy, LTK,
  YouTube transcripts). Never invent takeaways from a Hard source you couldn't
  read; say it was unavailable this week.

---

## 1. Cassandra Sethi — Next Level Wardrobe
- **Platforms:** Blog https://nextlevelwardrobe.com (Open) · YouTube (Partial) · Instagram @nextlevelwardrobe (Hard)
- **Principles to extract:** intentional capsule building; more outfits from fewer items; fit/fabric/finish over trend; neutral core palette; polished-but-not-corporate professional wear.
- **Weight:** Anchors the overall "polished business casual" direction. Rarely changes the filter — confirms the lane.

## 2. itsolgavi (Olga V) — Threads & Tribulations
- **Platforms:** Substack https://itsolgavi.substack.com (Open) · YouTube (Partial) · ShopMy/LTK (Hard) · Instagram/TikTok (Hard)
- **Principles to extract:** fabric & construction scrutiny; resale value and secondhand-as-value-path; skepticism of luxury markup without ethics/quality; short supply chains, repairability; brand deep-dives. Notably flags **Lafayette 148 as underpriced on resale** — bias toward it secondhand.
- **Weight:** High for quality/value judgments and which brands to hunt secondhand.

## 3. Andrea Cheong — Mindful Monday Method
- **Platforms:** Book *Why Don't I Have Anything to Wear?* · YouTube/TikTok (Hard) · secondary coverage (Open via articles)
- **Principles to extract:** read the care/composition label first (treat it like an ingredients list); prefer natural fibers; inspect seams, lining, buttons, finishing; distrust "premium" marketing without construction evidence; judge the exact garment, not the brand halo.
- **Weight:** High as a quality gate. Every pick should pass a Cheong-style fabric/construction check.

## 4. Tinsley Crisp — The Everygirl
- **Platforms:** Everygirl articles https://theeverygirl.com (Open) · Instagram (Hard)
- **Principles to extract:** refresh existing closet with accessories before buying basics; scarves (neck/bag/belt), statement flats, textured/raffia bags, restrained pattern, playful-but-polished color; relaxed trousers + tee + light layer; boxy/cropped shirt or vest + relaxed trousers; breathable pull-on pants.
- **Weight:** Expansion/whimsy lens only — **small dose**. Never overrides the quality/fabric/fussiness filters. Whimsy must stay polished, never boho/novelty/juvenile.

## 5. Derek Guy — Die, Workwear! (NEW)
- **Platforms:** Blog https://dieworkwear.com (Open) · Substack https://dieworkwear.substack.com (Open) · Bluesky @dieworkwear.bsky.social (Partial) · Instagram @die_workwear (Hard)
- **Note:** He covers **menswear**. Use him two ways:
  - **(a) Principles lens — applies to every pick:**
    - Fit & proportion over trend; judge the garment on the body, not the label.
    - Cost-per-wear is the value test; favor versatile pieces that earn many wears.
    - Cloth first — weight, weave, hand, and fiber over branding or "premium" marketing (reinforces Cheong + itsolgavi).
    - Skepticism of both fast fashion and unjustified luxury markup.
  - **(b) Translated picks — menswear → women's equivalents:** when he praises a
    fabric/silhouette, translate the *concept* into a women's search, e.g.
    high-twist tropical wool trousers → women's high-twist wool trousers;
    oxford-cloth button-down → women's OCBD; flat-front vs pleat & rise guidance
    → applied to women's trouser silhouettes; soft natural-shoulder tailoring →
    unstructured women's blazers. Translate concepts, never surface his exact
    menswear product links.
- **Weight:** High for quality reasoning and silhouette/proportion; medium for
  translated product ideas (interpret carefully, always re-check against the
  style guide).

## Source-handling rules
- Read `personal-style-guide.md` first; it wins any conflict with a source.
- Prefer the most recent posts; note publish dates.
- If a source is Hard/Partial and unreadable this week, **say so in the report**
  and base nothing on it. Honesty over coverage.
