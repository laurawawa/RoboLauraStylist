# Personal Style Guide

Last updated: 2026-06-09

## Style North Star

Polished business casual with quiet quality, natural fibers, and a little Parisian ease. The best pieces feel intentional but not stiff: tailored trousers, crisp or relaxed button-downs, refined knits, low-profile shoes, and simple jackets. Prefer pieces that can work for a professional setting without looking corporate or fussy.

Influences:
- Cassandra Sethi / Next Level Wardrobe: high-quality, intentional wardrobe, polished professional outfits, simple outfit formulas.
- itsolgavi: fabric and construction awareness, resale value, quality deep dives, skepticism toward overhyped brands.
- Andrea Cheong: care-label scrutiny, item-by-item quality judgment, skepticism toward "premium" marketing without construction evidence.
- Tinsley Crisp / The Everygirl: attainable but aspirational styling, workwear refreshes, outfit formulas, accessories, trend-aware sourcing, and small touches of whimsy through an NYC stylist lens.
- Sezane: feminine but wearable Parisian basics, especially trousers and knits.

## Preferred Categories

Prioritize:
- Trousers and pants first.
- Button-down shirts and refined woven tops.
- Boxy woven summer tops and non-white shirting.
- Simple jackets only when they fill a real gap.
- Low-profile shoes that support polished casual outfits.

Deprioritize for now:
- Blazers, unless exceptional.
- Dresses, unless highly versatile.
- Sweaters and knitwear.
- Decorative tops with heavy ruffles, bows, or dramatic trim.

## Fit And Size Notes

Likely sizes:
- US 8 in pants and tailored pieces.
- Sezane FR40 / US8 in trousers.
- Medium in shirts, knits, and relaxed tops.
- EU 38.5-ish in shoes, with US 8 appearing in purchase history.
- Treat M / US 8 women's sizing as the default target. Do not recommend S/XS or fuzzy conversions unless measurements strongly support it.

Fit preferences:
- Trousers: wide-leg, straight-leg, ankle-length, pleated, or polished pull-on if fabric is substantial.
- Shirts: relaxed but not sloppy; Barry/Eileen-style button-downs are good references.
- Avoid pants that are sheer, clingy, or flimsy.
- Avoid statement buttons when they dominate the piece.

## Colors And Prints

Core neutrals:
- White, ivory, ecru, cream.
- Sand, camel, tan, beige.
- Navy.
- Black.
- Soft gray.

Accent colors that work:
- Pastel yellow.
- Olive/sage if muted.
- Mocha/champagne.
- Pale blue.
- Tobacco if softened.

Prints:
- Polka dots are a yes when the scale and color feel elegant, like the Sezane Terrence pant.
- Ecru/navy tennis stripes are a yes.
- Burberry nova check can work if the item is simple and not loud.
- Tinsley Crisp filter: small doses of trend, color, accessories, texture, or whimsy can be useful when they make existing basics feel current, but they still need to look polished and wearable.
- Avoid overly colorful novelty prints, loud florals, and anything that reads boho/costume.

## Fabrics

Strong yes:
- Linen, especially substantial linen or linen blends that are not sheer.
- Cotton poplin, oxford, or crisp shirting cotton.
- Wool and wool blends for trousers.
- Silk and silk-linen blends when washable/care is acceptable.
- Merino wool for knits.

Andrea Cheong filter:
- Read the fabric tag first; treat composition like an ingredients list.
- Prefer natural fibers and natural-feeling blends over synthetic-heavy "premium" marketing.
- Check seams, lining, buttons, and finishing before trusting brand reputation.
- Judge the exact garment, not the halo of the brand.

Conditional:
- Viscose/rayon only if the cut and brand are excellent; itsolgavi influence means scrutinize this.
- Performance linen/cotton-linen blends when the listing still looks polished.

Avoid:
- Polyester-heavy pieces.
- Thin white linen pants without lining or clear opacity.
- Synthetic fast-fashion blends unless there is a specific reason.

## Origin And Quality Filters

Preferred origin:
- Made in USA.
- Made in Italy.
- Made in Portugal.
- Made in France, Romania, Switzerland, or other European countries when quality is clear.

Avoid:
- Made in Asia for this wardrobe search brief, unless the user explicitly relaxes this.

Always verify:
- Ask for fabric and country-of-origin tag photos when the listing does not show them.
- Treat marketplace titles as clues, not proof.
- Confirm "Ralph Lauren Purple Label" versus cheaper Ralph Lauren lines. Do not assume all Ralph Lauren is Purple Label.

## Price Guardrails

Default cap:
- Under $300 per item.

- It is a favorite brand.

- The item fills a clear wardrobe gap.
- The user explicitly approves stretching budget.

Best value zone:
- Shirts: $40-$150.
- Pants: $60-$225.

## Buying Checklist

Before recommending:
- Confirm item page link is a direct listing, not a category/search page.
- Confirm price is under $300 unless noted.
- Confirm size is US 8, FR40, or M depending on item.
- Confirm natural fiber content from title, description, or tag photo.
- Confirm origin is USA or Europe, or mark as "ask seller to confirm."
- Prefer summer-weight pieces over transitional/fall fabric.
- Do not recommend white, cream, ivory, ecru, or other light neutral pants unless the user reopens that category.
- Avoid fussy details: ruffles, loud buttons, heavy embellishment, novelty prints.
- Prefer pieces that make at least three outfits with existing wardrobe themes.

## Outfit Formulas

Easy defaults:
- Navy or charcoal trouser + pale blue or sand shirt + low-profile white trainer.
- Stone trouser + tobacco or muted stripe shirt + flat sandal/loafer.
- Wide-leg trouser + tucked non-white linen shirt + black ankle boot.
- Crisp summer trouser + simple woven top + minimal leather sandal.
- Burberry check cotton piece + otherwise plain neutrals.

## Do Not Recommend Unless Asked

- More white/ivory blazers similar to the existing blazer.
- White pants.
- Cream, ivory, ecru, or other light neutral pants.
- Duplicate white linen button-downs.
- Sweaters or cardigans during summer resale scans.
- See-through linen pants.
- Pants with conspicuous buttons.
- Ruffle-front shirts.
- Very expensive Sezane/Ralph Lauren pieces over $300.
- Items where "Ralph Lauren Purple Label" is ambiguous or likely mislabeled.
