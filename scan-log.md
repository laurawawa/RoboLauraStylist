## Carried-over learnings (durable preferences, distilled from the Codex era)

These are the standing buying preferences as of the migration. They live in
`personal-style-guide.md` too; repeated here as the quick-reference for runs.

- **Size:** M / US 8 / FR40. Don't recommend S/XS or fuzzy conversions without
  measurements.
- **Pants — currently covered / closed:** no white, cream, ivory, ecru, or other
  light-neutral pants; no more navy slacks; cream/light-neutral category is full.
- **Tops:** pass on additional *solid-color* button-downs for now; prefer
  patterned/textured-but-polished woven tops and non-solid shirts. **No black
  button-downs.**
- **Knits:** no sweaters/cardigans during summer scans.
- **Season:** summer = linen / cotton / light natural-fiber trousers first; wool
  is lower priority and avoid meaningful polyester blends.
- **Quality gate:** natural fibers; read the composition label; inspect
  seams/lining/buttons/finish; verify USA/European origin or flag "ask seller."
- **Whimsy (Tinsley):** small-dose only — scarves, statement flats, textured/raffia
  bags, restrained pattern. Never boho/novelty/juvenile.
- **Verification:** strict live-link checking; reject sold/ambiguous/stale
  listings aggressively. Numbered output.
- **Excluded sources:** TOAST, LEMAIRE, The Vivienne Files, Permanent Style.
- **Don't re-recommend (pending):** Banana Republic Everyday Linen Shirt,
  Heathland Brown, size M (offer was made on Poshmark).
- **Strong positive signals:** Lafayette 148 (also flagged by itsolgavi as
  underpriced resale); COS & Madewell strongest of the mall/minimalist brands;
  Apiece Apart gray wide-leg linen-cotton pants; brown/tan/tobacco woven shirts.

### Feedback (2026-06-13)
Laura reviewed all 4. Curation was on-target but she bought nothing — none rose
to must-have. Item-by-item:
1. **Lafayette 148 espresso brown pinstripe linen trousers** — likes them, not
   buying right now. → Keep brown/tobacco patterned linen trousers as a strong
   direction; she's selective, so only surface trousers that are genuinely
   compelling or gap-filling.
2. **Madewell blue mixed-stripe linen shirt** — not a fan of the stripe design.
   → Ease off busy/multi-stripe shirting; favor other patterns/textures
   (small check, subtle weave, restrained print) over stripes for tops.
3. **J.Crew leopard calf-hair flats** — loves the whimsy, but doesn't need more
   shoes right now. → **Shoes category covered**; deprioritize statement
   flats/shoes until she reopens it. Leopard/whimsy still a yes in principle.
4. **Burberry nova-check silk scarf** — pretty, doesn't need a scarf right now.
   → **Scarves/accessories not needed right now**; deprioritize until reopened.

**Net direction for next run:** fewer, higher-conviction picks. Lead with
trousers and non-stripe patterned/textured tops in her open lanes. Hold off on
shoes and scarves/accessories. Whimsy stays welcome but applied to needed
categories, not add-ons.
